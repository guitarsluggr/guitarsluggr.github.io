Preface & Recommended Specs

Off the bat, I would NOT recommend doing this unless you have an explicit reason to switch to Linux. This does not significantly change performance in a meaningful way.

If you do want to do this, I highly recommend installing Linux on a new and separate hard drive. Partitioning part of an existing hard drive that has Windows installed on it is asking for a Windows update to corrupt or even completely wipe your Linux OS partition.

Doing this in linux means you are relying on graphics card support in linux, thus running into potential issues if there is a gap in support vs the Windows drivers. Considering this, I recommend the following as min specs:

  • 10 GB VRAM on your GPU
  • 3080/4070ti or better(or AMD Equivalent or better)
  • 32 GB RAM

The graphical issues you could face if you do not meet these specs could be exascerbated because of the Linux environment.

ReFramework



Download ReFramework from one of the following two locations:
Nexus: https://www.nexusmods.com/monsterhunterwilds/mods/93?tab=files
Github: https://github.com/praydog/REFramework-nightly/releases/

Next, drop the "dinput8.dll" file into your MH Wilds installation folder. Can find this by right clicking on MH Wilds in your steam library, and going to "Browse Local Files...".

Next, drop the "dinput8.dll" file into your MH Wilds installation folder. Can find this by right clicking on MH Wilds in your steam library, and going to "Browse Local Files...".

Lastly, depending on if you want to also include ReShade, copy one of the following Launch Options. You will paste it into:
Steam > MH Wilds > Right Click > Properties... > General > Launch Options



ReFramework ONLY Launch Options:

WINEDLLOVERRIDES="dinput8.dll=n,b" %command%



ReShade ONLY Launch Options:

WINEDLLOVERRIDES="d3dcompiler_47=n;dxgi=n,b" %command%



ReFramework & ReShade Launch Options:

WINEDLLOVERRIDES="dinput8.dll=n,b;d3dcompiler_47=n;dxgi=n,b" %command%



ReFramework & ReShade Launch Options with optional parameters:

PROTON_HIDE_NVIDIA_GPU=0 PROTON_ENABLE_NVAPI=1 WINEDLLOVERRIDES="dinput8.dll=n,b;d3dcompiler_47=n;dxgi=n,b" %command%

ReShade



Install the Proton ReShade here: https://github.com/kevinlekiller/reshade-steam-proton

Full installation instructions can be found here: https://guides.martysmods.com/reshade/installing/reshademanualshaderinstall/



Once you have ReShade installed, there are 2 more steps. The first is to set up the launch options, which can be found in the ReFramework section.

The second is to download any dependencies (ReShade Shaders and Textures). I recommend starting with a preset. Many presets can be found on Nexus, and I also have my colorful preset here.

To download a preset, I dont know the "correct" way... but the way I do it is download the individual fx files from here:
https://github.com/Mortalitas/GShade/blob/master/Shaders/Colourfulness.fx

Find whatever presets you're missing in the navigation menu on the left, download them, and then place them in the appropriate Shaders or Textures folder in your MH Wilds installation location.

My ReShade folder location looks like:

/home/guitarsluggr/.steam/debian-installation/steamapps/common/MonsterHunterWilds/ReShade_shaders/Merged



OBS



If you want a Replay-like feature, I went with OBS. After you download it, make sure you set up the bitrate of your recording to an appropriate level of quality.

Then, once you have it set up, you can follow this guide to do replays with OBS:
https://www.xda-developers.com/how-turned-obs-shadowplay-replacement/
