Preface & Recommended Specs

Off the bat, I would NOT recommend doing this unless you have an explicit reason to switch to linux. This does not significantly change performance in a meaningful way.

If you do want to do this, I highly recommend installing linux on a new and separate hard drive. Partitioning part of an existing hard drive that has Windows installed on it is asking for a Windows update to corrupt or even completely wipe your Linux OS partition.

Doing this in linux means you are relying on graphics card support in linux, thus running into potential issues if there is a gap in support vs the Windows drivers. Considering this, I recommend the following as min specs:

  • 10 GB VRAM on your GPU
  • 3080/4070ti or better(or AMD Equivalent or better)
  • 32 GB RAM
The graphically issues you could face if you do not meet those could be exasperated because of the linux environment.
